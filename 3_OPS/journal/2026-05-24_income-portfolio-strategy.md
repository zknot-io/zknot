# Income Portfolio Strategy — Bridging the 3-Month Runway Without Sacrificing the Moat

**Date:** 2026-05-24
**Author:** William Shane Wilkinson
**Context:** Pre-revenue, ~3-month runway (even selling zero PowerVerify / ZKKey / HashStamp). Goal: generate enough income to extend runway 3mo → 6mo+ WITHOUT derailing the high-value build work (ITV white paper due June 3, CMMC enclave, ZKNOT core). This entry locks the income plan and the guardrails.

---

## The decision in one line

Run a **two-engine income plan hedged against platform risk**: Seeking Alpha (gatekept authority/compounding) tested fast and cheap first, HashStamp (owned channel) built deliberately in parallel — with a write-once chip build-guide as the product asset, consulting as warm-lead-only, PowerVerify protected, and ALL income capped beneath the ITV + CMMC build.

## Finish line & constraints (from elicitation)

- **Finish line:** "Just enough trickle to stretch 3mo → 6+." Modest, defensive. NOT a swing-for-the-fences need.
- **Hours:** Founder said "whatever it takes" — but see guardrail below. With 3mo of buffer, going all-in on income is the wrong move; it would starve the exact build work this whole analysis says is highest-value.
- **Tiebreaker (founder's instinct):** ranked "highest ceiling" #1. CORRECTED in discussion: for *bridge income* sized to a trickle, "highest ceiling" is a trap (pulls toward overbuilding a second startup). The operative tiebreaker for side income is **reinforces-the-moat + sustainable**, NOT ceiling. Ceiling is the right instinct for ZKNOT itself, not for the bridge.

## Why two engines (the key insight)

Not indecision — **platform risk hedge.** Seeking Alpha is a *gatekept* channel: open to everyone, but every article is editorially reviewed and can be declined (confirmed: "editors can be quite picky"). That acceptance is NOT in the founder's control. HashStamp is an *owned* channel — no gatekeeper. Running both means no single rejection/failure sinks the income plan. Trader's instinct: don't put liquidity in a position whose exit you don't control.

## The locked plan (sequenced by cost-to-test)

1. **Seeking Alpha — TEST FIRST (fast, cheap, low energy).** Answer the "will they accept me" question early because it's days of effort, not weeks, and draws on *finished* expertise (day-trading yrs + accounting + econ + MBA). Apply as contributor, submit ONE article on a **public, no-COI** cyber / attestation-adjacent company. If accepted → compounding authority spine + market intel feeding SBIR commercialization. If declined → lost only days, learned the gatekept channel is closed, reallocate to owned channels.
   - **Terms (verified 2026-05-24):** Program open to everyone, no invite/credential needed; only requirement = article exclusive to SA. All submissions editorially reviewed/selectable. Pay = per-pageview on premium/exclusive articles (historically modest: tens–few hundred $/article); real money is Pro/Marketplace tiers requiring track record. So: trickle that compounds, NOT fast cash.
   - **COI FLAG (important):** Founder runs a company in the exact sector. Write about LARGE PUBLIC players where there's clearly no competition and no position held — NOT small-cap adjacents where the line blurs. Disclosure obligations apply; stricter if writing about anything held/traded. "Think before publishing" zone. (Not legal advice.)

2. **HashStamp.io — owned-channel hedge, built deliberately in parallel.** GATE before sinking build hours: *is it chargeable THIS month with what already exists, or does it need finishing?* If chargeable now → low-touch self-serve SaaS income, great. If it needs building → it's a ZKNOT build item, NOT income; pace it accordingly. Either way it's the channel no one can reject the founder from.

3. **Chip build-guide / provisioning SOP — write-once product asset (triple currency).** Sells as a product + doubles as ZKNOT's own provisioning doc + builds SBIR/teaming technical credibility. Likely content: FT260 USB-I2C bridge (UMFT260EV1A) + bare TrustCUSTOM provisioning, TFLXTLS slot config. Slow build, turns to trickle by month 2–3 (matches timeline). **Keep OUTSIDE the ZKNOT umbrella for liability** — verify the separation is real (separate entity or clear disclaimers, no shared IP claims). Lawyer question.

4. **Consulting (CMMC/cyber to small contractors) — WARM-LEAD-ONLY, unscheduled.** Fastest cash on the menu and deepens the CMMC moat, but founder ranked it last and the 3mo buffer means fast cash isn't needed. Take it if a CMMC-panicked contractor lands in the lap (post–Nov 2025 enforcement = lots of them); do NOT prospect.

5. **PowerVerify / ZKNOT core IP — PROTECTED. Do not touch.** No pre-launch social marketing (IP-disclosure timing risk against the provisional), no fire-sale. Crown-jewel / moat asset, not grocery money.

## Governing guardrail (the thing that protects everything)

**Income work is capped beneath and subordinate to ITV (June 3) and the CMMC build.** The 3-month runway EXISTS so the moat can be protected — spending the runway's whole purpose chasing income the founder has time to grow slowly is the core mistake to avoid. "Whatever it takes" applies to the MOAT, not to side income. Recommended hard cap ~10–15 hrs/wk on income; income work never displaces an ITV or CMMC deadline. Two engines tempt overcommitment — hold the line that Seeking Alpha is a fast cheap *test*, not an open-ended writing job, until proven accepted.

# Courtroom Evidence Rules + Hardware Attestation — Working Reference

Durable reference material for ZKNOT's PUF/Federal-Rules-of-Evidence intersection. Synthesis lives in the journal entry; the durable "how does X work" facts live here so they're grep-able from the vault.

## 1. Mental model

The American evidence system is built around one anxiety: **the jury must not be fooled by something that sounds authoritative but is wrong.** Every rule below is plumbing for that anxiety.

Evidence enters trial through a layered gauntlet. Each layer asks a different question. Cryptographic provenance interacts with three of these: relevance, authentication, and (for AI-touched output) reliability. The other layers (hearsay, best evidence) are mostly orthogonal to ZKNOT's work.

| Layer | Rule(s) | Question | ZKNOT relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relevance | 401–403 | Does this make a fact more/less likely? | Mostly orthogonal |
| Authentication | 901, 902 | Is this what its proponent claims it is? | **Primary** — PUF proves device identity |
| Hearsay | 801–807 | Out-of-court statement for truth? | Mostly orthogonal |
| Best Evidence | 1001–1008 | Original or duplicate? | Tangential — crypto duplicates routinely accepted |
| Expert / Reliability | 702, proposed 707 | Reliable principles, reliably applied? | **Secondary** — when AI is in the capture or post-processing path |

## 2. Federal Rule of Evidence 901 — Authentication

**Text of 901(a):** *"To satisfy the requirement of authenticating or identifying an item of evidence, the proponent must produce evidence sufficient to support a finding that the item is what the proponent claims it is."*

**Threshold is deliberately low.** Judge doesn't decide authenticity conclusively — decides whether a reasonable juror *could* find it authentic. This is Rule 104(b) conditional relevance.

**901(b) is a non-exhaustive list of examples** of how to satisfy 901(a). The one that matters for ZKNOT:

**901(b)(9) — Process or System.** Authentication by describing *"a process or system used to produce a result and showing that the process or system produces an accurate result."*

This is the existing on-ramp for cryptographic capture systems. PUF-based attestation can be authenticated under 901(b)(9) by laying foundation: describe the silicon, the PUF challenge-response, the signing protocol, the verification math. Costs a witness fee per case — Rule 902 is the path to *self-authentication* (no witness required).

### Proposed Rule 901(c) — Deepfake Challenge Burden Shift

Released alongside Rule 707. Creates a procedure when an opponent challenges electronic evidence as fabricated by AI.

**Key mechanic:** Once the opponent makes a *prima facie* showing that evidence is AI-generated, the **proponent must establish by a preponderance of the evidence** that it is genuine. This raises the bar from "reasonable juror could find authentic" to "more likely than not authentic."

**Engineering implication:** A PUF-derived signature whose verification math is independently reproducible from public parameters is the cleanest possible "preponderance" showing. The opponent argues "this could be fake"; the proponent responds with a verification that succeeds if and only if the data came from a specific physical silicon device at a specific moment.

Status: Advisory Committee may finalize a version in Spring 2026 alongside Rule 707.

## 3. Federal Rule of Evidence 902 — Self-Authenticating Evidence

Rule 902 lists 14 categories that need no extrinsic authentication at all. Most are traditional (certified public records, notarized documents). Two are modern and critical:

- **902(13) — Certified records generated by an electronic process or system.** Added 2017. Qualified-person certification → no live witness needed.
- **902(14) — Certified data copied from an electronic device or storage medium.** Added 2017. Hash-value comparison + qualified-person certification → self-authentication.

**Procurement-relevant insight:** 902(13)/(14) were specifically designed to *reduce the cost of authenticating digital evidence*. Pre-2017, introducing a body-cam video meant flying the records custodian. Post-2017, a certification plus hash check can do the same job at near-zero marginal cost.

**Cost reduction is the procurement story.** ZKNOT's value to a state AG or DA's office isn't just "better security" — it's "cheaper, faster, more defensible self-authentication of every piece of evidence your agency produces."

902 currently does not list "PUF-attested capture" as a self-authenticating category. But 902(13)/(14) are the template for future categories. A future 902(15) or 902(16) for hardware-attested capture is plausible within ~5 years. Being the reference implementation when that rule lands is the long game.

## 4. Federal Rule of Evidence 702 — Expert Testimony / Daubert

Governs expert testimony. Current text (post-2023 amendment) requires the proponent to demonstrate, *more likely than not*, four things:

- **(a)** The expert's specialized knowledge will help the trier of fact
- **(b)** The testimony is based on sufficient facts or data
- **(c)** The testimony is the product of reliable principles and methods
- **(d)** The expert has reliably applied the principles and methods to the facts

This is the **Daubert standard**, from *Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals*, 509 U.S. 579 (1993). Made trial judges gatekeepers of expert reliability. Pre-Daubert, junk science walked into court. Post-Daubert, judges actively exclude unreliable methodology.

### Daubert reliability factors (non-exclusive, canonical list)

- Has the method been tested?
- Has it been subjected to peer review and publication?
- What is its known or potential error rate?
- Are there standards controlling its operation?
- Is it generally accepted in the relevant scientific community?

### Why this matters for ZKNOT

Every Daubert factor maps cleanly to well-studied cryptographic primitives. SHA-256, ECDSA, the underlying math of ring oscillator and SRAM PUFs — published error rates, NIST standards, decades of peer review, broad community acceptance.

**A PUF-based attestation method built on standard primitives is structurally easier to Daubert-qualify than novel AI forensic tools.** Quiet but enormous advantage when the courtroom contest is your boring crypto vs. an opponent's AI-detection-of-AI tool.

## 5. Proposed Federal Rule of Evidence 707 — Machine-Generated Evidence

**Proposed text:**

> Rule 707. Opinion Evidence Generated by a Machine or Process.
> When machine-generated evidence is offered without an expert witness and would be subject to Rule 702 if testified to by a witness, the court may admit the evidence only if it satisfies the requirements of Rule 702(a)–(d). This rule does not apply to the output of simple scientific instruments.

**Plain English:** If an AI system produces output that, *if a human expert said it*, would need to pass Daubert, then the AI output also needs to pass Daubert — even without a human expert on the stand.

### What this catches

- AI-enhanced surveillance video
- Facial recognition matches
- License-plate-reader hits
- AI report-writing tools (e.g. Axon Draft One)
- Predictive risk-scoring outputs
- AI transcription
- AI redaction

### What this explicitly does not catch

"Output of simple scientific instruments" — thermometers, breathalyzers, basic measurement tools. The boundary is fuzzy and will be litigated. Body-camera raw capture is probably not a "simple scientific instrument" but is also not really "machine-generated opinion." Where AI features are layered onto the capture (real-time translation, scene tagging, automatic redaction), 707 absolutely bites.

### Timeline (memorize)

- **Aug 16, 2025** — Revised Rule 707 published for public comment

**Sales-cycle implication:** Even before formal effective date, courts cite proposed rules in discovery disputes and Daubert hearings. The argument isn't "the law requires this today" — it's "the law is visibly converging here, and you do not want to be caught short."

## 6. The interaction — how the rules compose at trial

A defense attorney challenging body-cam evidence post-707 has a clean playbook:

1. **Rule 901 challenge** — "This video has not been authenticated as actually originating from the device claimed." Survivable with custodian testimony + hash verification.
2. **Proposed 901(c) deepfake challenge** — "We have reason to believe this video could have been generated or altered by AI." Proponent must now make a *preponderance* showing. Hash chains don't answer this (they prove the file didn't change after capture, not that the capture was real).
3. **Rule 707 challenge on AI features** — "The transcription was AI-generated; the redaction was AI-applied; the scene-tagging was AI-generated; the proponent has not laid Daubert foundation for each model." Survivable but expensive.
4. **Rule 403 challenge** — "Probative value is substantially outweighed by danger of misleading the jury given the unsettled state of underlying AI methodology." Wraparound argument.

**The ZKNOT response that collapses all four:** A verification protocol that, given only public parameters and the file, returns YES/NO on the question "did this originate from a specific physical capture device whose PUF identity is registered in this trust anchor?" If YES:
- Move 1 fails (device identified cryptographically)
- Move 2 fails (capture authenticity affirmatively established)
- Move 3 narrows to only the post-capture AI features (raw original preserved with attestation intact)
- Move 4 has no traction because the methodology is standard cryptography with published error rates

This is not a marketing claim. This is the actual architecture ZKNOT has to build and defend.

## 7. Defense Layer Stack — where each technology sits

| Layer | What it is | What it stops | What still kills it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0. Naked metadata** | EXIF, file timestamps | Honest mistakes | Any deliberate adversary in 30 seconds |
| **1. Software signing** | App-layer signature over file content | Storage-layer tampering | Key extraction from app; sensor injection; OS compromise |
| **2. C2PA / Content Credentials** | Standardized manifest, software-signed | Provenance loss; uninformed editing | Same as Layer 1 if signing key isn't hardware-protected |
| **3. TEE / secure enclave signing** | Key lives in TrustZone/SGX/SE; sensor data signed in TEE | Software key extraction | Sensor injection at hardware bus; lab-grade physical attacks |
| **4. Hardware Root of Trust + attested boot** | TEE + measured boot + remote attestation | Runtime tampering | Sensor injection; FIB/SEM lab attacks; manufacturer-trust assumptions |
| **5. PUF-derived identity + capture-path attestation** | Key is a physical property of silicon; capture path attested | Key extraction (no key to extract); device cloning; supply-chain key implant | Sensor injection IF no sensor-path attestation; protocol-level attacks |

### Where the industry is right now

- **C2PA (Layer 2):** Adobe, Microsoft, Truepic, BBC, Sony cameras. Useful as a provenance standard. Vulnerable to metadata stripping in social-media reprocessing and to signing-key compromise.
- **Sony/Leica/Nikon hardware-signed C2PA (Layer 3):** State-of-the-art in pro photography. Key in secure element. Vulnerable to sensor injection (demonstrated in research).
- **Axon's stack (mostly Layer 1, partial Layer 2):** Merkle-tree hash at capture, SHA-2 on upload. No public claim of hardware-bound signing keys. No public C2PA. No public capture-path attestation.
- **PUF-based capture authentication (Layer 5):** Almost nobody in production for evidence. PUFs used widely for chip authentication (Maxim, Intrinsic ID/Synopsys, Microchip Trust&GO); courtroom-evidence application is genuinely uncrowded.

## 8. The Sensor-Injection Attack — the single most important talking point

**Setup:** Attacker wants a video accepted by a trusted device's signing pipeline. They generate synthetic video with a diffusion model. They obtain a trusted capture device. They do not need to extract keys or compromise the OS.

**Attack:** Off-the-shelf HDMI-to-CSI-2 (or equivalent) bridge injects the synthetic feed into the device's image-sensor-input bus. The device's ISP, OS, and signing app have no way to distinguish "frames from the CMOS sensor" from "frames injected at the bus." The device signs the synthetic frames as legitimate capture.

**Why this defeats Layers 1-4:** Every layer above PUF-with-sensor-attestation trusts the device to deliver real sensor data. None proves the data came from the physical sensor. Signing happens *after* the bits enter the device's main pipeline; injection happens *before* that point.

**Why PUF alone is not automatically enough:** A PUF-derived key that signs whatever the pipeline hands it is no better than a TEE key signing the same thing. PUF beats sensor injection only if the PUF system *also* attests the sensor path:
- Cryptographic challenge to the sensor itself, OR
- Physical sealing of the sensor-to-PUF path, OR
- Integrated sensor + PUF die, OR
- Equivalent mechanism

**If ZKNOT cannot articulate how its system defeats sensor injection, it's selling Layer 3 with extra steps.** This is the architectural test for any "we use PUFs" claim.

### Self-rating checklist (do this honestly)

- [ ] Is the signing key derived from a PUF (never stored, recovered at use)?
- [ ] Is the PUF on a certified secure element with documented characterization?
- [ ] Does the protocol bind the captured data to the specific device identity?
- [ ] Does the protocol bind the captured data to a specific time / counter?
- [ ] Is there a story for sensor-path attestation against bus injection?
- [ ] Is the verification math reproducible from public parameters alone?
- [ ] Is there a published threat model document?
- [ ] Is the protocol resistant to ML-modeling attacks on the PUF?
- [ ] Is there a key revocation / device retirement story for compromised units?
- [ ] Does the audit trail integrate with FRE 902(13)/(14) certification workflows?

**Anything below 6 YESes → claiming Layer 5 but shipping Layer 3. Fix the gaps before pitching to any serious cryptographer.**

## 9. PUF Taxonomy — vocabulary for cryptographer conversations

| PUF type | Mechanism | Maturity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SRAM PUF** | Power-on state of SRAM cells | Most common, most studied; NIST-acceptable | Intrinsic ID (now Synopsys) commercial product; Microchip Trust&GO uses this |
| **Ring Oscillator PUF** | Manufacturing variations in RO frequencies | Mature; common on FPGAs | Good for FPGA targets |
| **Arbiter PUF** | Race condition between two paths | Older | **Vulnerable to ML-modeling attacks (Rührmair et al.).** If used, must address ML-modeling resistance |
| **Optical / Coating PUF** | Physical scattering pattern of a coating layer | Niche | Used for chip-package authentication |
| **Memory-based (DRAM, Flash)** | Various flavors | Mixed | Stability and characterization trade-offs |

**ZKNOT's current path:** Microchip Trust&GO / TFLXTLS = SRAM PUF on the ATECC608 platform. Defensible, mature choice. The right framing in a cryptographer conversation: *"We use a commercial SRAM PUF in a NIST-certifiable configuration; the novelty is in the protocol layer and the integration with verification infrastructure, not in inventing new PUF physics."* Schneier-tier reviewers respect "boring crypto in a novel application" over "we invented new crypto."

## 12. Key courtroom terminology cheat sheet

- **Attestation** — Cryptographic evidence that a specific state or computation occurred on a specific device.
- **Authentication** — Proving an item of evidence is what its proponent claims.
- **Chain of custody** — Documented sequence of who handled evidence, when, and how.
- **Daubert hearing** — Pretrial reliability hearing on expert evidence under Rule 702.
- **Daubert standard** — The reliability gatekeeping standard from *Daubert v. Merrell Dow*.
- **Deepfake** — Synthetic media from generative models depicting events that didn't occur.
- **Liar's dividend** — Bad actors successfully challenge real evidence as fake because fakes are now possible (Citron & Chesney 2019).
- **Machine-generated evidence** — Output of a computational process offered as evidence; the subject of proposed Rule 707.
- **Self-authentication** — Evidence admissible without extrinsic foundation (Rule 902).
- **Sensor injection** — Attack where adversary injects synthetic media into a trusted capture device's sensor input bus.

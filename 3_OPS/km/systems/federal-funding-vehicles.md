# Federal Funding Vehicles — Reference

Durable mechanics for the non-SBIR funding landscape, promoted out of the
2026-05-27 funding-strategy journal. **Verification flag:** figures below
(match ratios, dollar bands, dates, CMMC deadlines) come from secondary sources
dated ~Jan–May 2026. Before relying on any eligibility detail for an actual
submission or filing, confirm at the primary source listed.

---

## OTA — Other Transaction Authority (10 U.S.C. 4022)

The legal basis that lets DoD award prototype projects *outside* the FAR. Built
for non-traditional/commercial entrants and dual-use tech. A prototype OT can
convert to a **follow-on production contract or agreement on a sole-source basis,
with no further competition** — this is the structural prize behind DIU and the
consortia. Award types under CSOs are limited to fixed-price / fixed-price-incentive.

## CSO — Commercial Solutions Opening (DIU's front door)

- Competitive procedure to award prototype OTs for commercial/dual-use tech.
- **Three phases:** (1) Solution Brief — short whitepaper / brief / pitch video,
  *not* a full proposal; (2) Pitch; (3) full written proposal + negotiation, only
  if invited (Request for Prototype Proposal / RPP).
- AOIs (Areas of Interest) post against a standing CSO; respond only when an open
  AOI genuinely matches the stack.
- **Speed:** DIU targets 60–90 days selection→award (current avg closer to ~120);
  full CSO→kickoff ~3–6 months. Faster than SBIR (6–12 mo) and DARPA BAAs (6–18 mo).
- **Reality:** highly competitive; far more briefs than they can fund. Needs a
  *genuine* current military-need match, not a theoretical one. A government
  end-user who wants you sharply raises odds.
- Monitor: diu.mil/work-with-us/open-solicitations (subscribe / RSS).
- **ZKNOT fit:** strong when an AOI touches cyber / zero trust / supply-chain
  integrity. Entry barrier (short brief) is low; the bar is need-match + champion.

## Consortium-managed OTAs (NSTXL family)

- NSTXL manages multiple OTA consortia; the two named here are **S2MARTS**
  (Strategic & Spectrum Missions Advanced Resilient Trusted Systems) and **SpEC**
  (Space Enterprise Consortium).
- **Membership is the gate:** only active members can submit proposals to S2MARTS /
  SpEC solicitations and view full solicitation docs in the member portal.
- **Dues:** ~$250–$10,000/yr, scaled by entity type and revenue; a single fee
  covers all NSTXL OT consortia. Pre-revenue ZKNOT sits at/near the floor (~$250–500).
- **CMMC gate:** NSTXL members must have a **CMMC Level 2 self-assessment (or higher)
  recorded in SPRS by Nov 10, 2026**. Some opportunities may also require a Level 1
  self-assessment. The L2 self-assessment is a months-long lift from cold — scope early.
- **S2MARTS focus** = advanced microelectronics, COTS programmable-IC co-development,
  RAD-HARD / strategic-missions hardware, trusted systems. **This is the closest
  thematic home for hardware-rooted attestation.**
- Economics (illustrative, from secondary analysis): dues are trivial vs award sizes;
  the binding variable is *engagement* — writing solution briefs that beat the
  downselect. Pay-and-don't-engage = zero return.
- Primary sources: nstxl.org, info.nstxl.org, aida.mitre.org (OTA consortia map),
  space-enterprise.org.
- **ZKNOT fit:** best thematic match (S2MARTS microelectronics/trusted hardware),
  low cost. Watch the CMMC L2 deadline. Join with a target AOI, not on spec.

## STRATFI / TACFI (AFWERX/AFVentures) — BLOCKED for ZKNOT

Valley-of-death bridges from SBIR/STTR Phase II → Phase III scaling. **Not standalone
vehicles.**
- **Eligibility (all required):** small-business concern eligible for SBIR/STTR; on an
  active SBIR/STTR Phase II or completed one within 2 years of capability-package
  submission; the subject Phase II not already given a sequential PII; ≥90 days since
  Phase II execution began.
- **Submission requires a Government POC** (end-user) to complete the ingest form —
  not submittable by the company alone. Mandatory templates.
- **TACFI:** $375K–$2M SBIR funds over 2 yrs; match ≥ 1:1 (SBIR : other-gov OR private).
- **STRATFI:** $3M–$15M over 4 yrs; match for every $1 SBIR → $2 other-gov, OR
  $1 other-gov + $2 private. Internal/affiliated funds don't count; third-party must be
  outside cash investors.
- Primary source: afwerx.com STRATFI/TACFI pages + the PY26.x Notice of Opportunity docs.
- **ZKNOT status: OFF the table** — no SBIR Phase II. Same root blocker as SBIR.

## AFWERX SBIR Open Topic — BLOCKED (program-wide)

Truly open dual-use program (industry/tech-agnostic), normally a fast first-award path
via pitch days. **Currently frozen: AFWERX cannot issue new SBIR/STTR awards until
Congress reauthorizes the program.** Existing awards unaffected; new entrants waiting.
A topic pivot won't help until reauthorization. Primary source: afwerx.com / SBIR.gov.

## IRAD — Independent Research & Development (not an application)

A prime's **own overhead R&D money**, partially DoD-reimbursable, that primes spend
maturing tech they expect to need. **You do not apply for IRAD.** You convince a prime
that your tech de-risks something on *their* roadmap, so they spend their own dollars
integrating/testing you, or name you in a proposal. Pure relationship + technical-credibility
play. The ICF / VA session is the closest live IRAD-adjacent shot.

## Tradewinds Solutions Marketplace (TSM) — LIVE, but scope stretch

- DoD (CDAO + Applied Research Institute) marketplace for **AI/ML, digital, and data**
  solutions. Run via Army Contracting Command–Rock Island.
- **Entry = a single ≤5-minute pitch video** through a Solutions Provider account. No
  incumbent advantage, no past-performance threshold. Designed to lower barriers for firms
  with little federal history.
- **Assessment within ~30 calendar days;** "Awardable" status = pre-vetted, sole-source-
  purchasable by any DoD buyer through rapid pathways (placement ≠ guaranteed award).
- Monthly collection windows; recurring open call renewed by revision. **Current window
  closes ~May 31, 2026 — verify the next collection period on SAM.gov / tradewindai.com.**
- Read the announcement + evaluation rubric (Appendix B) before recording.
- **ZKNOT fit:** the verification backend + ZK audit trail can be framed as "digital/data
  assurance," but TSM is AI/ML-scoped — this is a defensible stretch, not a clean fit. Lowest
  effort/cost of any live path; treat as opportunistic, not primary.

## Cross-cutting insight

Every fast vehicle resolves to a **named government or prime champion**, not a form: DIU
needs an end-user, STRATFI needs a Gov POC, IRAD *is* a prime relationship, consortium OTAs
trace to a sponsoring program office. The binding constraint is sponsorship, not paperwork.

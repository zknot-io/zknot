# ZKKey → Product Rename — Naming Brief

**Prepared:** 2026-06-08 · **Scope:** the flagship strong-form device currently called *ZKKey* (and its *Connect* / *Air* variants) · **Status:** options + recommendation; nothing cleared for use yet

---

## Why rename

You're moving the flagship into a professional, evidentiary market — lawyers, journalists, newsrooms, election and forensic bodies, agencies. Two problems with the current naming for that audience:

- **"ZKKey"** reads as crypto-native jargon. "ZK" signals zero-knowledge/blockchain to insiders and reads as noise to everyone else; "Key" undersells it (it's not a login key — it's a witness to human authorization).
- **"StrongFormGate / HardwareGate"** are internal engineering tokens, not a product name.

The name a newsroom or general counsel sees on a device that seals evidence should explain itself in one beat and sound trustworthy.

## What a good name has to do here

1. **Self-explain to non-crypto professionals** — the device proves *a real human authorized this, after seeing what they signed*. The best names evoke a **witness / notary / seal**, not "crypto."
2. **Carry evidentiary gravitas** — it ends up in chain-of-custody, court, and newsroom contexts.
3. **Be ownable** — distinctive enough to trademark in Class 9 (your TM class) and weakly contested. *Plain dictionary words in this space (Proof, Witness, Seal, Attest, Verify) are descriptive, crowded, and hard to own — distinctiveness is what's marketable AND protectable.*
4. **Extend to a family** — needs `[Name] Connect` (tethered) and `[Name] Air` (air-gapped) to work, sitting beside PowerVerify, TrustSeal, and verifyknot under ZKNOT.
5. **Stay inside your marketing guardrails** — no absolute-claim names (avoid "Proof," "Certain," "Sure," "Lock") that overpromise security.

## Candidates by strategy

**A — Notary / witness (says the function in plain professional language) — strongest direction**
- **Knotary** *(Knot + notary)* — a notary witnesses that a real person signed, when, and vouches for it: exactly the device. Brand-coherent with ZKNOT, instantly meaningful to lawyers/journalists, distinctive, ownable.
- *Attest / Attestor* — accurate but descriptive and crowded (see scan).
- *Avow, Aver* — "to declare/assert as true," short and serious; less crowded but more abstract. *(Not yet cleared.)*

**B — Knot / binding lineage (maximum brand coherence with ZKNOT)**
- *Cinch* (to make certain; also a binding), *Hitch*, *Tether*, *Anchor*. Warm and concrete, but most are common words with heavy existing use. *(Not yet cleared; expect collisions.)*

**C — Seal / mark of authenticity (evidentiary gravitas)**
- *Cachet* (an official seal; connotes authenticity + prestige) — on-theme and elegant, but a common word, hard to own.
- *Sigil* (a mark that authorizes) — evocative and short, but reads slightly mystical/gamer, less "mature professional."
- *Seal, Signet* — descriptive/crowded. *(Not yet cleared.)*

**D — Provenance / custody (evidence-chain framing)** — **avoid:** crowded in your exact Class 9 lane (Provenance Technologies; E-Provenance).

**E — Keep a family marker (if you want the ZKNOT tie explicit)**
- *ZK Sigil, ZK Witness, Keymark* — keeps lineage but reintroduces the "ZK" jargon you're trying to outgrow. Use only if family signaling outweighs broad-market clarity.

## Recommendation

**Lead: Knotary.** It's the one name that is simultaneously self-explaining to your new audience (a notary witnesses and vouches), brand-coherent with ZKNOT (the *knot* binds a human to a signature), distinctive enough to own, and clean in a first-pass search. It extends cleanly: **Knotary Connect** (tethered) and **Knotary Air** (air-gapped), with ZKKey retired to internal history.

- **The honest caveat (both sides):** the pun is its strength *and* its risk. It's memorable and meaningful — but the most conservative government buyers may read "clever" before "serious." Mitigations: present it in a clean wordmark with an evidentiary tagline ("Witnessed signing. Independently verifiable."), and let the device's gravitas carry it. If after seeing it in context the pun still feels wrong for your primary buyer, the fallback is a distinctive non-dictionary alternative from Strategy A/C (e.g., *Avow* or *Cachet*), cleared first.
- **Why not the safe dictionary words:** Witness/Seal/Attest/Proof are the obvious "serious" picks, but they're descriptive, largely unprotectable, and already worn — they'd make you *less* distinctive in exactly the market where trust is the product.

## Decisions for you (these set the direction)

1. **Family marker or clean break?** Keep a "Knot"/"ZK" tie (Knotary keeps *knot*), or go fully independent?
2. **Tone:** evidentiary-serious, or approachable-professional? (Knotary leans approachable-but-credible; Avow/Cachet lean austere.)
3. **One flagship name + Connect/Air modifiers** (recommended), confirmed?

## Next steps once you pick

1. Formal trademark knockout search (Class 9 + 42) and counsel clearance.
2. Secure domains + handles before any public use.
3. Update the tracker short name, the File Naming Master registry, device-ID prefixes (e.g., ZK-K/ZK-A → new), and brand docs in `7_ENG/`.
4. Decide ZKKey's retirement: hard rename, or keep as an internal/engineering codename through the current production batch.

# PowerVerify — Earned-Media & Outreach List

**Goal:** Free / earned coverage for PowerVerify before paying for marketing.

**Core rule:** Do not pitch a "launch." Pitch a *story* (to journalists) or a *build* (to makers).
The product is the proof, not the headline. Tailor every journalist email — no blast templates.

**Recommended order:** Community-first → accumulate proof/buzz → then story-led journalist pitches.

---

## ⚠️ Pre-send check (do this once, before anything goes out)

- [ ] The claim "construction is verifiable / signed, tamper-evident record" matches **exactly** what
      verifyknot.io renders, and the correct attestation tier (SELF-ASSERTED vs ZKNOT-PROVISIONED) is named.
- [ ] A public "how it works" page exists that a technical reader can poke at (Hackaday/HN will).
- [ ] A teardown photo set + the verify mechanism are ready to send on request.

## Community channels — your actual free-marketing engine

These are where the "juice-jacking complainers" and pentesters already are. Higher signal, lower risk,
and the proof you build here is what makes a journalist pitch credible later.

- **Hacker News** — Show HN: post the build/how-it-works page (not the store). Expect scrutiny of the
  verifiability claim; that's the point.
- **r/netsec** — strict, technical-only; a writeup of the verification mechanism fits, a product ad does not.
- **r/hardware**, **r/privacy**, **r/cybersecurity**, **r/homelab** — the cable's audience.
- **lobste.rs** — small, very technical; good for the attestation/crypto angle.
- **Tindie** (Hackaday-adjacent maker marketplace) — natural home for a verifiable-hardware product.
- **Infosec Mastodon (infosec.exchange)** — where most of the journalists above actually hang out;
  engage authentically *before* you ever email them.

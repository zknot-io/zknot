# ZKNOT Provisioning — EPISODE 1 SCRIPT
## "You Can't Screenshot Your Way to Proving You Made Something"
### The why. ~7 min · Shoot AFTER E2.

_Companion to `zknot-provisioning-video-scripts.md`. Same format. VO is scaffolding — rewrite in your voice. This is the thesis episode: keep every abstract point anchored to something concrete on screen, or it drifts into a TED-talk nobody finishes._

**Format key:** 🎬 VISUAL · 🖥️ SCREEN · 🎙️ VO · 📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT · 💡 VIGNETTE · 📚 REF

---

### SCENE 1 — COLD OPEN: the four-second fake [0:00–0:35]
🎬 VISUAL: Tight on your screen. A clean, official-looking "✓ Certified Authentic — issued to [name], [date]" badge / certificate.
🖥️ SCREEN: You select it, duplicate it, change the name and date in an image editor. Fast. Casual. Done in seconds.
🎙️ VO: "That took me about four seconds. A certificate, a verified badge, a timestamp — anything you can see on a screen, you can copy, edit, or fake. So here's a question I couldn't stop thinking about: if all of our proof can be faked that easily… how does anyone actually prove they made something?"
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT: _(none — let the edit land)_
💡 VIGNETTE — **the four-second fake:** the whole problem in one gesture. Don't narrate over it too much; let the viewer feel how trivial it was.

---

### SCENE 2 — TITLE / WHO / SERIES FRAME [0:35–1:15]
🎬 VISUAL: Title card → you on-cam or VO over bench B-roll.
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT: **ZKNOT · Hardware Trust, Made Accessible · Ep.1 — The Why**
🎙️ VO: "I'm [Shane], I'm building ZKNOT. If you caught the last one, you watched me wrestle a one-dollar chip awake on a fifteen-dollar bridge. This is the episode underneath that one — *why* I'm doing any of it. Why a physical chip is the answer to a problem that's about to get a lot worse."
💡 VIGNETTE: frame the series as one question asked five ways. "Five videos, one question: how do you prove something is real?"

---

### SCENE 3 — WHY DIGITAL PROOF FAILS [1:15–2:45]
🎬 VISUAL: Rapid B-roll of the things we treat as "proof" — a screenshot, an email-to-self, a PDF certificate, a cloud "verified" checkmark.
🎙️ VO: "Think about what we actually rely on. A screenshot — copyable. An email you sent yourself — editable, and the timestamp's just text. A PDF certificate — anyone with the template can mint one. A 'verified' badge on some platform — that only means whoever runs that server *says* so. Every one of these comes down to the same thing: trust me. Trust the platform. Trust that nobody edited it."
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT (stamping over each): **copyable · editable · mintable · "trust me"**
🎙️ VO: "And in 2026, 'trust me' is collapsing. A machine can generate a photo, a document, a voice, an entire identity, on demand. The gap between 'real' and 'looks real' has basically closed."
📚 REF: (description) the content-provenance problem — industry efforts like C2PA / content credentials exist for exactly this. _Verify current C2PA scope before stating specifics on camera; reference it at the landscape level._

---

### SCENE 4 — THE NEAR-MISSES (be fair to them) [2:45–4:00]
🎬 VISUAL: Three quick cards, each with a ✓ and an ✗.
🎙️ VO: "Now, smart people have built real answers — and I want to be fair about what they get you, because none of them are wrong, they're just incomplete for what I care about."
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT + 🎙️ VO, three beats:
- **Notarization** — ✓ a trusted human vouches. ✗ slow, costs money, doesn't scale to a thousand things a day, and you're trusting the notary.
- **Blockchain timestamp** — ✓ proves *something* existed at a time, publicly. ✗ proves the data existed — not that *you*, or your *device*, are the one who made it. Authorship is still open.
- **Cloud 'verified'** — ✓ convenient, instant. ✗ whoever controls the server controls the truth. Centralized trust, single point of failure.
🎙️ VO: "Each one closes part of the gap. None of them anchors the claim to a *physical thing that can't be copied.* That's the missing piece."
💡 VIGNETTE — **"show both sides":** this scene is your credibility. You're not trashing alternatives to sell yours; you're showing you actually understand the landscape. That honesty is the brand.

---

### SCENE 5 — THE SHIFT: anchor it in atoms [4:00–5:15]
🎬 VISUAL: Pick up the secure-element chip from E2. Hold it to camera.
🎙️ VO: "Here's what changes everything. Inside this chip is a secret key. Not a password you type — a key that is generated inside the chip and physically cannot be read back out. Not by me. Not by you. Not by someone who steals the chip."
🖥️ SCREEN: simple animation — data goes *in*, a signature comes *out*, the key stays *inside*, glowing, never leaving.
🎙️ VO: "What it *can* do is sign things. And because only this one physical chip holds that key, that signature is proof that *this specific thing in the real world* vouched for it. You've moved your proof out of 'trust me' and into atoms. You can't screenshot a thing that proves itself by existing."
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT: **The key never leaves. The signature is the proof.**

---

### SCENE 6 — THE PADLOCK AND THE VAULT [5:15–6:00]
🎬 VISUAL: Two objects, literal or graphic — a small padlock, a heavy vault.
🎙️ VO: "There are two tiers of this, and I'll spend the rest of the series on both. There's the padlock — the dollar chip, cheap enough to put on everything, which is what most people and small teams actually need. And there's the vault — the serious hardware that guards the master keys everything else trusts. Same idea, two scales."
💡 VIGNETTE — **padlock/vault, full introduction:** this is the metaphor's home episode. Plant it clearly; E2/E3/E4 all call back to it.

---

### SCENE 7 — THE PART THAT'S ACTUALLY MINE [6:00–6:45]
🎬 VISUAL: You, direct, lower energy, honest. The genuine-curiosity beat.
🎙️ VO: "Here's the thing that bugs me, and it's why I'm building this in public. This technology isn't new. Banks have it. Governments have it. It's in the phone in your pocket. But it's locked behind enterprise budgets and NDAs and 'call our sales team.' Nobody shows the rest of us how. And if proving you made your thing is about to matter to everyone — every inventor, every artist, every small shop — then it can't stay a thing only the big players get. That's the whole project: make hardware-rooted proof something *you* can actually do."
💡 VIGNETTE — **why you care:** this is your personal monopoly stated plainly. It's the most important 45 seconds in the episode. Say it the way you'd say it to one person, not a camera.

---

### SCENE 8 — WRAP + SERIES MAP [6:45–7:15]
🎬 VISUAL: Pull back to the bench; the chip, the bridge, hint of the vault.
🎙️ VO: "So that's the why. From here: I provision the padlock — the cheap chip — then go up to the vault, then show how they fit together, and finally tear apart what 'certified' even means, because that part's a mess. Full writeups at zknot.org. Come build with me."
📺 ON-SCREEN TEXT: **The series → zknot.org · Subscribe**
💡 VIGNETTE: close on the cold-open callback — "And no — you still can't screenshot it."

## E1-specific production notes
- **Keep it short.** The why earns its keep by being sharp, not long. If a scene drifts abstract, cut it or anchor it to something on screen.
- **Scene 7 is the heart.** Everything else can be competent; that one has to be *real*. Don't script your way out of your own voice there.
- **Watch-order independence.** E2 ships first, so E1 references it lightly ("if you caught the last one") without depending on it. A new viewer landing on E1 still gets a complete thought.
- **Accuracy guardrail (Scene 3 ref):** the content-provenance/C2PA landscape is real and worth referencing, but verify its current scope before any specific on-camera claim — same accuracy-is-brand rule as the rest of the series.
- **No IP exposure here.** E1 is conceptual; it stays at the level of public mission and never touches the novel mechanism. Safe to publish freely.
